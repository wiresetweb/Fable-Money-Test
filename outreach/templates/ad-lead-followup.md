# Ad-lead follow-up email sequence

Companion to `strategy/ad-plan.md`. These are for people who come in **through the Meta
ad funnel** (submitted the `/tools/` form, or replied to a retargeting ad). Different from
the cold-outreach template (`base-electrician-quote-tool.md`) — these people raised their
hand, so the tone is warmer and faster, not a cold intro.

**Rules (per `claude.md`):** every send is personalized with researched specifics, makes no
invented claims, comes from Laz's real identity, and carries a one-line opt-out. Bracketed
`[...]` parts MUST be replaced before sending. Save each real send to `outreach/sent/`.

The goal of the whole sequence is one thing: **get a reply.** Not to close in an email — to
start a conversation Laz can close.

---

## Email 0 — Instant auto-acknowledge (automated)

Set up as a Web3Forms auto-response (or a one-tap saved reply Laz fires the second a lead
lands). Its only jobs: confirm receipt, set the speed expectation, and signal "real human."

**Subject:** Got your message — Wireset Web

> Thanks [First name] — got your note about [business name].
>
> A real person (me, not a bot) will get back to you within one business hour with a quick
> idea for what we'd build you. If it's after hours, first thing in the morning.
>
> While you wait, here's the live demo again — it takes 30 seconds on your phone:
> https://wiresetweb.com/demos/tools/quote-calculator/
>
> — [Laz], Wireset Web

## Email 1 — The personal reply (within 1 business hour) ⟵ the one that matters

Speed is everything. This is a real, specific, human reply. Reference their actual trade and
the pain they described in the form.

**Subject:** Re: [business name] — quick idea

> Hi [First name],
>
> Thanks for trying the calculator. Based on what you said — [echo their pain in their words,
> e.g. "quotes are all phone calls right now"] — here's what I'd build for [business name]:
>
> [One concrete sentence describing THEIR tool — e.g. "a quote calculator on your site where
> a homeowner picks 'AC repair' or 'new system, 3-ton' and instantly sees your price range,
> then it lands in your inbox as a job request with their address and phone."]
>
> It's **$350 flat, built around your actual services and prices, and live within 48 hours**
> of a deposit. You own it — no monthly fee, no contract.
>
> One honest note: that price and timeline cover a single, focused tool. If what you need
> turns out to be bigger — multiple user logins, syncing with other software, lots of moving
> parts — I'll tell you that up front and quote it fairly instead. No surprises either way.
>
> Want me to mock it up with a few of your real services in it? Reply with the 3–4 jobs you
> quote most and I'll show you. If now's not the time, just say "not now" and I'll leave it.
>
> — [Laz]
> Wireset Web · wiresetweb.com · [phone]

## Email 2 — The concrete nudge (+2 days, no reply)

Make it real by showing, not asking. Lower the effort to almost zero.

**Subject:** Mocked this up for [business name]

> Hi [First name], no pressure — I went ahead and sketched what your quote tool could look
> like using a few typical [trade] jobs:
>
> [2–3 example lines, e.g.
>  • "Service call / diagnostic — $89"
>  • "AC tune-up — from $129"
>  • "New system install — request a quote"]
>
> Your real version would use your exact services and prices, and every submission would hit
> your phone as a job request. Same deal: $350, 48 hours, you own it.
>
> Want me to turn this into a working demo with your name on it? Just reply "yes" — takes me
> a day. Or "not now" and I'll stop here.
>
> — [Laz], Wireset Web

## Email 3 — Handle the objection (+4 days)

Name the doubt he's not saying out loud. Trades have been burned; meet it head-on.

**Subject:** The catch (there isn't one, but here's the honest version)

> Hi [First name] — figured I'd answer the question I'd be asking: *what's the catch on
> $350 in 48 hours?*
>
> Straight answer: it's a single-purpose tool, not a giant platform — so it's a day or two
> of focused work, not a six-week agency project. I use AI for the grunt work and handle the
> judgment calls myself, which is exactly why it's $350 instead of $5,000. You get a real
> working link, you own the files, and there's no monthly anything.
>
> If it turns out you need something bigger, I'll quote that honestly too. But for "stop
> texting prices at 9pm," the simple version does the job.
>
> Worth a quick look with your services in it? Reply and I'll build the mockup.
>
> — [Laz], Wireset Web

---

## Email 4 — The break-up (+7 days) ⟵ usually the highest reply rate

Permission to say no gets more yeses than another pitch would.

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

> Hi [First name] — I don't want to keep landing in your inbox if the timing's wrong.
>
> If you'd still like to see what a quote tool (or a faster website) would do for [business
> name], just reply and I'll pick it right back up. If not, no hard feelings — reply "close
> it" and you won't hear from me again.
>
> Either way, thanks for checking us out.
>
> — [Laz], Wireset Web

---

## Email 5 — Delivery + upsell + referral (post-sale)

The most valuable email in the set: turns one $350 sale into a website lead and the next
customer.

**Subject:** Your tool's live 🎉 + two quick things

> [First name] — your [tool] is live: [link]. Quick guide attached; you can change your
> [prices/items] yourself anytime as we covered.
>
> Two things while I've got you:
>
> 1. **The website.** This tool lives on your site — and honestly, a faster, sharper site is
>    where it really pays off (it's our main line of work). If you ever want to look at that,
>    say the word and I'll put together options. No push.
>
> 2. **A favor.** If you know one other [trade] owner who's still quoting by phone, I'd be
>    grateful for the intro — and I'll take great care of them.
>
> Thanks for trusting us with this. It was a pleasure to build.
>
> — [Laz], Wireset Web

## Honesty checklist before every send
- [ ] Personalized with real, observed specifics (not a blast)
- [ ] No invented results, testimonials, team, or history
- [ ] Price/timeline claims only on standard-scope tools (complexity valve respected)
- [ ] One-line opt-out present
- [ ] Sent from Laz's real identity; copy saved to `outreach/sent/`
