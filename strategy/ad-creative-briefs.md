## 0. Read this first — why the first round of creative failed (and the rule that fixes it)

The placeholder creatives were **text on a navy gradient**. In a feed, that reads instantly as "an ad," and the trades audience scrolls past anything that looks designed-to-sell. The fix is one rule that governs everything below:

> **Don't make an ad. Make a thumb-stop.**
> Real photography of the trade. The actual product on a real phone. Native, almost "scrappy" framing. One idea, huge text, legible at a glance. The brand stays in the corner, not the spotlight.

Polished beats nothing, but **authentic beats polished** for this audience. When in doubt, make it look like a contractor's buddy posted it, not like an agency made it.

## 1. The assignment

- **What we sell (in these ads):** a **$350, 48-hour custom quote tool** for local service businesses (the wedge). Secondary: the websites.
- **Who sees it:** owner-operators of **HVAC, electrical, plumbing** shops (1–10 trucks). See the full persona in `ad-plan.md` §1 — short version: *"Mike," 43, quotes jobs from his phone at 9pm, distrusts marketers, decides in 3 seconds and one thumb.*
- **The one job of every creative:** stop his scroll and earn the tap to `wiresetweb.com/tools/`. Not to explain everything — to provoke "wait, what's that?" and get the click.
- **Tone:** plain, confident, a little blunt. Talks like a tradesperson, not a brand.

## 2. Brand kit — set this up in Canva *before* designing

Create a Canva **Brand Kit** with these exact values so every export is on-brand.

**Colors**
| Role | Hex |
|------|-----|
| Navy (primary bg) | `#0B1A2E` |
| Deep navy (gradient bottom) | `#081427` |
| **Amber (the one accent)** | `#F5A524` |
| Amber hover/bright | `#FFB83A` |
| Amber-ink (text on amber) | `#6B3D00` |
| Ink (near-black) | `#0F172A` |
| Off-white | `#F7F7F4` |
| White | `#FFFFFF` |

**Fonts** (Canva equivalents of the site's system stack)
- **Headlines:** `Archivo` **Black/Bold**, or `Inter` **Bold** — tight letter-spacing, near-black or white. Big and confident.
- **Body/caption:** `Inter` Regular/Medium.
- **The two-tone trick (signature):** in a headline, set the payoff word/clause in **amber**, the rest in white/ink. e.g., "Get your price. **Right now.**"

**Logo** (files provided — upload to Brand Kit)
- `logo-lockup-white.png` → for dark/photo backgrounds.
- `logo-mark-amber.png` → the W+bars mark alone, for tight corners.
- `logo-appicon-navy.png` → navy square + amber mark.
- Keep the logo **small, in a corner.** It's a sign-off, not the hero.

**Shape & usage rules**
- Buttons/tags = **full pill** (fully rounded). Cards = 14px radius.
- **Amber is ~5% of any frame** — one accent (a word, an underline, a pill), never a big fill.
- Plenty of contrast; never amber text on amber.

**Do / Don't**
- ✅ Real photos of trades, hands, trucks, panels, the product on a phone.
- ✅ One headline, huge, ≤6 words on the image.
- ✅ Native framing (looks like a post / a screenshot / a text message).
- ❌ Gradient-only backgrounds with centered text (the failed look).
- ❌ Stock "business handshake," clip-art, multiple competing messages.
- ❌ Tiny text, busy collages, more than one CTA.

## 3. Placement & size matrix (build every concept in these)

Meta serves different ratios in different spots. Produce each greenlit concept in **all four**, designing for the ratio (not just cropping).

| # | Ratio | Pixels | Placement | Priority | Safe zone notes |
|---|-------|--------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **4:5** | **1080 × 1350** | FB & IG **feed** (tallest allowed — owns the most screen) | ⭐ **Primary** | Keep key text/logo out of the **bottom ~250px** (headline + CTA UI sits there). |
| 2 | **1:1** | **1080 × 1080** | Feed, Marketplace, Explore, Audience Network | ⭐ High | Center the idea; works as the universal fallback. |
| 3 | **9:16** | **1080 × 1920** | **Stories & Reels** (huge cheap reach) | ⭐ **Primary** | Keep all content between **top ~250px** and **bottom ~420px** (profile bar top, CTA/caption bottom). |
| 4 | **1.91:1** | **1200 × 628** | Right column, in-stream, Audience Network, link preview | Medium | Landscape — put subject one side, text the other. |

> Minimum legible headline height on mobile ≈ 60–70px. If a headline doesn't read at arm's length on a phone, it's too small.

## 4. The concepts

Five concepts. **A, B, and C are the lead horses** (one product-proof, one native pattern-interrupt, one founder video). D and E round out the test and the website cross-sell. Launch them as **separate ads** and let Meta find the winner — one idea per ad.

### CONCEPT A — "Their thumb, your prices" (product-in-context) ⭐
**The idea:** Show the actual quote tool *on a real phone, in a real hand, on a real job site.* Proof, not promise.

**Why it lands:** Mike has never seen a quote tool built for a shop like his. Seeing the real UI in a calloused hand on a job site does the selling — it's concrete, novel, and clearly *for him.*

**Visual direction:**
- **Hero asset:** the provided `asset-calc-filled.png` (the live Hartline Electric quote tool) composited into a **phone mockup** held in a **work-worn hand**. Canva: search a stock photo like *"hand holding phone outdoors construction"*, *"contractor holding smartphone"*, *"electrician hand phone van"*; drop the screenshot onto the phone screen (use a phone frame element).
- Background: softly blurred job-site context (HVAC condenser, electrical panel, work van interior). Navy-graded, not bright.
- **Amber accent:** a small pill bottom-left — `$350 · live in 48 hrs` — and the logo lockup small, top-left.

**On-image copy (minimal):**
- Headline overlay (top third): **"Quote it from the truck."**
- Sub-pill: `Your services. Your prices. Their thumb.`

**Meta text fields:**
- **Primary text (first line is the hook — ≤125 chars before "see more"):**
  *"Imagine a customer picking the job and seeing your price — without you typing a word. We build that, for your shop, in 48 hours. $350 flat, no monthly. Try a real one 👇"*
- **Headline:** `Your prices, on autopilot`
- **CTA button:** Learn more → `/tools/?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=tools_wedge&utm_content=A_thumb`

**Per-ratio adaptation:**
- **4:5 / 1:1:** phone-in-hand centered/right, headline top.
- **9:16:** phone fills most of the vertical frame (feels like they're holding it); headline top safe-zone, pill above the bottom safe-zone.
- **1.91:1:** phone-in-hand on the right third, headline + pill stacked left.

### CONCEPT B — "9:47 PM" (native text-message pattern interrupt) ⭐
**The idea:** A screenshot-style **text message** from a customer at night — "hey what would a new AC unit run me?" — that looks native in-feed, then flips to the point.

**Why it lands:** It doesn't look like an ad; it looks like a screenshot a friend shared. The 9pm pricing-text pain is universal and instantly recognizable. Pattern interrupt = scroll stop.

**Visual direction:**
- Recreate a **clean, generic** phone messaging UI (Canva: messaging/chat mockup element). Neutral contact name like *"Customer — Job Site"* and a timestamp **9:47 PM**. **Do not impersonate a real person or brand.**
- Incoming bubble: *"hey how much for a new AC unit? 😬"*
- Then the твist, as a bold caption **below/over** the screenshot on a clean off-white or navy band:
  **"What if they could just… see the price?"** (payoff word amber)
- Small footer: logo + `Custom quote tool · $350 · 48 hrs`.

**Meta text fields:**
- **Primary text:** *"It's 9:47pm. You're answering 'how much?' from your recliner again. There's a better way — a quote tool on your site that does it for you. $350, built in 48 hours 👇"*
- **Headline:** `Stop texting prices at night`
- **CTA:** Learn more → `…utm_content=B_text`

**Per-ratio adaptation:**
- **9:16:** the phone/message fills the top 60%, caption band across the lower third (inside safe zone). This is the **hero format** for this concept.
- **4:5 / 1:1:** message screenshot top, caption band bottom.
- **1.91:1:** message bubble left, caption right.

### CONCEPT C — "60 seconds, from the cab" (founder UGC video) ⭐
**The idea:** A rough, authentic **selfie-style video** (15–30s) — a real person (Laz, or a stand-in) talking to camera from a truck cab or shop, then a screen-recording of the live demo. Captions burned in.

**Why it lands:** For trades, founder UGC outperforms everything. It's a human, not a brand; it earns trust and feels native to Reels/Stories. The screen-record *shows the product working.*

**Format & priority:** **9:16 primary** (Reels/Stories), **1:1 secondary** (feed). Video.

**Shot list / script (hand to whoever records — phone camera is perfect):**
1. **(0–3s) Hook, to camera, no intro:** "If you're still texting customers prices at night — watch this."
2. **(3–12s) Screen-record of the live demo** (`/demos/tools/quote-calculator/`): pick a couple services, the price builds, hit send. Voiceover: "Your customer picks the job, sees your price, and it lands in your inbox as a real request."
3. **(12–22s) Back to camera:** "I'll build one around *your* services and prices. $350, live in 48 hours, you own it. No monthly anything."
4. **(22–27s) CTA:** "Tap below, try the real demo, takes 30 seconds." 

**Canva's role:** assemble the clips, burn in **animated captions** (Inter Bold, white with subtle navy stroke, amber for key words like **$350** / **48 hours**), add a 1.5s branded outro frame (navy, logo, `wiresetweb.com/tools/`), and a lower-third name tag. Keep cuts fast; don't over-polish.

**Meta text fields:**
- **Primary text:** *"30-second look at what we'd build your shop: a quote tool your customers use themselves. $350, 48 hours, no monthly. The demo in the video is live — try it 👇"*
- **Headline:** `See it working`
- **CTA:** Learn more → `…utm_content=C_video`

*(If no one can record yet: a captions-only version over the screen-recording alone still works — voice optional, text tells the story.)*

### CONCEPT D — "One job covers it" (price-objection / retargeting)
**The idea:** Kill the "is it worth it?" doubt with the math, over a real photo of the work.

**Why it lands:** Best as the **retargeting** creative for people who already saw the demo. Removes the only real friction (spending money) by reframing $350 as trivial.

**Visual direction:**
- Full-bleed real photo: an HVAC tech at a condenser / an electrician at a panel / a plumber under a sink (Canva stock: *"HVAC technician working"*, *"electrician panel"*). Navy gradient scrim on the lower half for text legibility.
- Headline over the scrim: **"$350 once. One service call covers it."** (amber on "$350")
- Small footer: logo + `Custom tool · live in 48 hrs · you own it`.

**Meta text fields:**
- **Primary text:** *"You checked out the quote tool 👀 — here's the math: it's $350 once, no monthly. One job it saves you pays for it. Want a mockup with your services in it?"*
- **Headline:** `Pays for itself in one job`
- **CTA:** Get a quote → `/tools/#contact?…utm_content=D_math`

**Per-ratio:** photo full-bleed in all; scrim + headline anchored to the safe-zone side (bottom for 4:5/1:1, lower-third for 9:16, one side for 1.91:1).

---

### CONCEPT E — "Your work is sharp. Your website isn't." (website cross-sell)
**The idea:** Side-by-side: the quality of their **craftsmanship** vs. the state of their **website**. Drives the website line (the bigger sale).

**Why it lands:** Every good contractor is quietly embarrassed by their 2019 site. This names it without insulting them — their *work* is the hero.

**Visual direction:**
- **Split composition.** Left/top: a crisp, proud photo of finished trade work (clean ductwork, a tidy panel, a finished bathroom). Right/bottom: a stylized "old website" frame — cramped, dated, a spinning-loader or "slow" cue. Clear visual gap in quality.
- Headline across the seam: **"Your work is sharp. Your website isn't."** (amber on "isn't" or "sharp").
- Footer: logo + `Fast sites you own — wiresetweb.com`.

**Meta text fields:**
- **Primary text:** *"When a customer Googles you, do they find work as good as what you actually do? We build fast sites for local trades — you own it, no rental traps. See our work 👇"*
- **Headline:** `Look as good as your work`
- **CTA:** Learn more → homepage `/?utm…utm_content=E_website`

**Per-ratio:** split left/right for 1.91:1 & 1:1; **stacked top/bottom for 9:16 & 4:5.**
