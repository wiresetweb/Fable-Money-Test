# The Offer

## One sentence
**A custom internal web tool for your business — built for you in 48 hours, $350 flat.**

## What "a tool" means (standard scope)
One single-purpose web tool that replaces a paper, phone, or spreadsheet process. Examples we lead with:
- **Instant quote calculator** — customer (or your tech) picks services, gets a price on the spot
- **Job tracker** — what's scheduled, in progress, waiting on parts, done
- **Booking / intake form** — customers request work online instead of playing phone tag
- **Inventory / materials sheet** — what's on the truck, what's low, what to reorder

## What's included at $350
- The tool, built custom around *their* services, prices, and workflow (not a template)
- Works on phones — that's where the trades live
- Hosted for them (free-tier infrastructure; no monthly fee from us for hosting)
- **One revision round** after delivery
- Delivered **within 48 hours of deposit**
- Plain-English handoff doc: how to use it, how to change prices/items themselves where possible

## Standard scope boundaries (what keeps it $350/48h)
A project qualifies for the flat price when ALL of these are true:
1. **One tool, one job.** It does one thing (quote, track, book, or count) — not a combination platform.
2. **One user role.** Everyone who uses it sees the same thing. No login tiers, no customer accounts.
3. **Simple data.** Their items/prices/jobs — not migrating years of history from another system.
4. **No deep integrations.** Email notifications and CSV export are in. Syncing with QuickBooks, a CRM, a payment processor, or custom APIs is out (quotable separately).
5. **One revision round** = a punch list of tweaks, not a redesign or new features.

## ⚠️ The complexity valve (non-negotiable, appears everywhere)
**If a request is bigger than standard scope, we say so before taking a dollar.** The exact line used on the site, in outreach, and in proposals:

> *"The $350 / 48-hour promise covers a single-purpose tool. If what you need is more complex — multiple user types, integrations, lots of moving parts — we'll tell you up front and give you an honest fixed quote and a real timeline instead. No surprises in either direction."*

Operationally: every inbound request gets scoped against the 5 boundaries above BEFORE Laz confirms price. If it fails any boundary, we write a custom fixed quote (see `pricing.md`) with a realistic timeline. We never start a non-standard build on the $350/48h promise.

## Payment terms
- 50% deposit ($175) via Stripe payment link before work starts — the 48-hour clock starts at deposit
- 50% on delivery, before final handoff of admin access
- Revision round happens after final payment (it's in the scope doc, so it's safe)

## Who it's for
Small local service businesses: electricians, plumbers, HVAC, auto shops, general contractors, landscapers, cleaners, mobile services. Specifically ones with an **observable** pain: paper quote forms, "call for pricing", phone-only booking, whiteboard job boards.

## Who we are (the honest version, for all copy)
A two-person shop: one human who handles the business side, one AI engineer doing the builds — which is exactly why a custom tool can cost $350 instead of $5,000 and ship in 48 hours instead of 6 weeks. We say this plainly; it's a feature, not a secret.

## The promise we must actually keep
48 hours from deposit to delivered tool, for standard scope. If we're ever at risk of missing it, Laz tells the client immediately and we offer a partial refund of the deposit. Reputation is the only marketing budget we have.
